> ### ðŸ’¡ Technical Note: Butter & Flour Precision
> * **Butter Tempering**: Butter must be softened enough to cut with a knife with minimal resistance. Avoid "soupy" or melted butter. If microwaving, use 5-second bursts only.
> * **Flour Aeration**: Pour flour into a separate bowl to fluff. Spoon into the measuring cup and level off with the flat edge of a butter knife. Do not pack.

## Instructions

- [ ] **Prepare Dry Base**: In a medium bowl, combine the precision-measured flour and crushed potato chips. Set aside.
- [ ] **Cream Base & Spices**: In a large bowl, combine softened butter, sugar, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Blend on medium-high until creamy.
- [ ] **Manual Integration**: Slowly add the dry ingredient mixture (flour/chips) to the wet base. **Switch to a mixing spoon and mix manually** until a dough forms.
- [ ] **Forming**: Roll dough into walnut-sized balls.
- [ ] **Placement**: Space balls 3 inches apart on parchment-lined sheets. Place on the middle oven rack.
- [ ] **First Stage Bake**: Bake for **XX minutes**.
- [ ] **Rotation**: Rotate the pan 180 degrees to ensure an even cook.
- [ ] **Final Stage Bake**: Continue baking for **XX minutes** until lightly browned.
- [ ] **Cooling**: Remove from oven and transfer directly to a baking rack.

## ðŸ›  Optional Patch: Cinnamon Sugar Dusting
For added texture and flavor, follow these steps once the cookies are **completely cooled**:

1.  **Prep Mixture**: Combine 2 tablespoons of sugar and 1 teaspoon of cinnamon in a gallon-sized freezer bag.
2.  **Coat**: Place up to 12 cooled cookies into the bag at a time.
3.  **Dust**: Gently shake the bag to coat all sides of the cookies. **Do not over-handle**, as the potato chip structure can make these cookies delicate.
4.  **Repeat**: Continue until all cookies are coated.
